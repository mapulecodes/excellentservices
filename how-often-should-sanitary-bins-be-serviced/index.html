How Often Should Sanitary Bins Be Serviced?

Service frequency determines hygiene compliance, odour control and cost. This guide helps Cape Town facility managers choose the right sanitary bin servicing schedule for their environment.

The Short Answer: It Depends on Usage Volume

There is no universal answer to sanitary bin service frequency — it depends on the number of female users accessing the facility each week, the type of environment (school, office, hotel, healthcare), and your regulatory obligations.

Overly infrequent servicing leads to full bins, odour and compliance failures. Overly frequent servicing is unnecessary cost. The right schedule balances hygiene, compliance and budget.

Monthly Servicing: Suitable For

Monthly servicing is appropriate for small offices with fewer than 40–50 female staff, low-traffic commercial properties with limited daily usage, and facilities where bins are rarely full before the end of a four-week cycle.

Monthly is the most common contract frequency and suits the majority of standard office environments in Cape Town.

Fortnightly Servicing: Suitable For

Fortnightly servicing is recommended for medium-sized offices (50–150 female staff), schools with regular term-time usage, clinics and medical rooms, and hospitality environments with moderate guest throughput.

Fortnightly servicing provides a meaningful reduction in overflow risk for facilities where a monthly schedule results in full or near-full bins at collection time.

Weekly Servicing: Suitable For

Weekly servicing is appropriate for high-traffic schools with large learner populations (500+ learners), hotels with high occupancy and frequent guest turnover, large retail environments, hospitals and clinics with significant patient volume, and any facility where bins consistently reach capacity before a fortnightly collection.

Schools: A Special Case

Western Cape government schools are subject to Department of Basic Education hygiene guidelines. A monthly schedule is the absolute minimum — many schools with large populations (800+ learners) require fortnightly or weekly servicing to remain compliant during term.

Importantly, some service providers reduce frequency during school holidays. Ensure your contract specifies term-time frequency separately from holiday periods, and that holiday deep-clean visits are included.
